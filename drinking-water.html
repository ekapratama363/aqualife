How Reverse Osmosis Transforms Contaminated Water Into Purity

As water enters the pipe, it carries impurities such as bacteria, viruses, and particles. The membrane acts as a barrier, allowing only clean water molecules to pass through, while trapping contaminants and pollutants. This process ensures that the water exiting the system is filtered and safe to use, effectively removing various pollutants and producing clear, clean water.
